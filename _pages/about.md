    <p>I am a doctoral student in cognitive systems neuroscience at the University of Oregon, advised by <a href="https://serenolab.uoregon.edu">Dr. Margaret Sereno</a>. My research focuses on visual perception, neuroaesthetics, complexity, self models, and altered states of consciousness.</p>
    <p>I am especially interested in how perception becomes meaningful experience: how visual structure, affect, prior belief, and context interact to make art, nature, and psychedelic states feel salient or transformative. I approach these questions through cognitive neuroscience, psychology, active inference, and computational accounts of perception.</p>
    <p>I am also a Fellow of the <a href="https://www.activeinference.institute/">Active Inference Institute</a>, where I contribute to interdisciplinary conversations in theoretical neuroscience. Previously, I worked as a clinical research coordinator in the <a href="https://medschool.cuanschutz.edu/psychiatry/research/faculty-labs/chaos-lab">CHAOS Lab</a> at the University of Colorado Anschutz, managing clinical trials on substance use and cognitive impairment, with a focus on cannabis and psychedelics.</p>
